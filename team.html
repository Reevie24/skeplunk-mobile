Skeplunk: Music Review Website

Skeplunk

Matt (MR): Co-founded Skeplunk with Billy. When he's not working on this review website he's either playing silly little games, watching trashy anime or working on his web development! Like he is right now with this website! :)

Billy (BG): Co-founded Skeplunk with Matt. When Karen Girdler was pregnant with Billy, she went to a Pink Floyd concert. 2 months later Billy was born on a rainbow surrounded by flying beds and lightning to the sound of Wish You Were Here. Raised on a healthy diet of Led Zepplin and guitar strings, he grew up to be a singer/songwriter, pianist, guitarist, bassist, drummer, ukuleleist, kazooist, DJ and pain in the arse to live with unless you’re deaf. He now spends his days reviewing rock, indie and electro albums on Skeplunk whilst living in a secret location three feet to the left of Area 51

Immy (IC): Hey there! My name’s Immy Chalk and I’m on mainstream pop rock!! So I’ll be reviewing any albums that fit into that particular category. Zain (ZR): Sup guys! I am the almighty Zain that’s plays da bass, guitar and a bit of singing. I grew up listening to a cluster of music when I was growing up from traditional Bollywood music to main stream music, my love of grunge and genres of that sort came from my cousins and friends who started to introduced me into Nirvana, SOAD etc. My favourite band would have to be the mighty Nirvana, Lower Than Atlantis and although I can’t really put my finger on a hated band or musician I suppose you could say the up themselves artists who claim to be all amazing and brilliant in everything they do such as 1 Erection etc. would be my main aim of hate. However on a lighter note my favourite musician would probably be Kurt Cobain, Mike Duce, Corey Taylor and Patrick Stump would by my all-time favourite musician and artists.

Sam (SJ): When not at the pub, Sam can usually be found in his cramped den of musical equipment, filled with musical instruments, old guitar parts and a pre-historic tape recorder. Determined to include guitars, mandolin, ukulele, banjo, bass, didgeridoo and whatever else he can get his hands on into a piece of music, he appreciates multi-instrumentalists. Anyone that is brave enough to include a banjo in a love song deserves respect; musicians like City and Colour, Newton Faulkner, Passenger etc. are high up there. Conversely, those who rely heavily on auto tune, overly done song structures and external lyricists deserve to be put in a musical wasteland of ill-repute. He is also in a band and likes to play wherever he can and with whoever is like-minded enough to join him.
